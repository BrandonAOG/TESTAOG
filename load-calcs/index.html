📖 How to Use This Calculator

Overview

Performs a Residential Optional Load Calculation per NEC 2020, Art. 220.82 for single-family dwellings. Complete Steps 1–5 in order, then view results on the Summary tab.

The Optional Method sums all loads, then applies a demand factor: first 10,000 VA @ 100%, remainder @ 40%.

Step 1 General Lighting, Small Appliance & Laundry

Enter the finished floor area (sq ft). NEC requires 3 VA/sq ft, minimum 2 small appliance circuits (1,500 VA ea.) and 1 laundry circuit (1,500 VA). These floors cannot be reduced.

Step 2 HVAC

Select CU/HP tonnage and supplemental heat for each AHU. The NEC uses the larger of: (a) heat element × 65% (≤4 units) or ×40% (>4 units) + blower @ 100%, vs (b) CU/HP load + blower @ 100%. The blower is never derated — always counts at 100%. Gas furnace = select "GAS + blower" so only the blower VA counts.

Step 3 Fixed Appliances

All permanently installed appliances. Rename, set qty, enter VA by selecting from the list or typing a custom value. Set voltage (120V or 240V) — this affects the neutral calculation. Gas checkbox removes the load. No separate demand factor (Optional Method). The Pool Equipment Panel row here represents the feeder VA going to the pool sub-panel.

Step 4 Dryers

Add as many dryers as needed. Check Gas to exclude any. Minimum 5,000 VA per unit per NEC. Type a custom VA if nameplate is larger.

Step 5 Cooking

Add cooktops and ovens/ranges individually. Each can be marked gas (excluded). Wattage selectable from 5,000–12,500 W in 500 W steps, or type a custom value. The "All Gas Kitchen" checkbox zeros all cooking at once. The raw VA is added directly to the demand pool — no Col C demand factor applies under the Optional Method (NEC 220.82).

Pool Panel Tab

Sizes the feeder from the main panel to the pool sub-panel. The largest continuous motor (pump or heater) is multiplied by 125% per NEC 430.24. 120V loads (lights) appear on Phase A only. All 240V loads split evenly. The pool sub-panel VA is also included in the main service demand via Step 3.

Conductor Sizing

Choose the feeder sizing table in Configuration. NEC 310.12 applies to 1&2 family dwelling services rated 100A–400A (uses the 83% loading column). NEC 310.16 (75°C) is the standard ampacity table used for all other feeders and branch circuits. The neutral is always sized from 310.16. Aluminum is code-compliant and common for service entrance conductors. The EGC is sized per NEC Table 250.122 based on the OCPD rating of the circuit it protects.

Feeder sets reduce the per-conductor ampacity required — e.g., 2 sets of conductors each carry half the load.

Generator

The minimum generator kW equals the service demand VA ÷ 1,000. LP output and NG output differ per model — select the correct fuel source on the Summary tab.

Neutral Demand

Per NEC 220.61: First 200A at 100%, above 200A at 70%. This is the maximum unbalanced load between the two hot legs and neutral.
